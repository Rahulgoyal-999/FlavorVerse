Mississippi Pot Roast Cheesesteak

A delicious fusion of classic Mississippi pot roast and Philly cheesesteak

Description

This Mississippi Pot Roast Cheesesteak combines the tender, flavorful meat of a classic Mississippi pot roast with the cheesy, savory elements of a Philly cheesesteak. The slow-cooked beef is shredded and served on a toasted hoagie roll with melted cheese, caramelized onions, and peppers.

Ingredients

  • 3-4 pound chuck roast
  • 1 packet ranch dressing mix
  • 1 packet au jus gravy mix
  • 1/2 cup butter
  • 6-8 pepperoncini peppers
  • 2 large onions, sliced
  • 2 bell peppers, sliced
  • 8 hoagie rolls
  • 16 slices provolone cheese
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Instructions

  1. Place the chuck roast in a slow cooker.
  2. Sprinkle the ranch dressing mix and au jus gravy mix over the roast.
  3. Add the butter and pepperoncini peppers.
  4. Cook on low for 8 hours or until the meat is tender and easily shreds.
  5. Remove the meat and shred it, discarding any large pieces of fat.
  6. In a large skillet, saut√© the onions and bell peppers until caramelized.
  7. Add the shredded meat to the skillet and mix well.
  8. Split the hoagie rolls and toast them lightly.
  9. Fill each roll with the meat mixture and top with 2 slices of provolone cheese.
  10. Place under the broiler until the cheese is melted and bubbly.

Tips & Notes

  • For best results, use a well-marbled chuck roast.
  • The meat can be cooked up to 2 days ahead and reheated.
  • Add extra pepperoncini peppers for more heat.
  • Try different cheese combinations like provolone and mozzarella.
