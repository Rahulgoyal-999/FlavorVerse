Homemade Alfredo Sauce

Prep: 5 mins
Cook: 15 mins
Total: 20 mins
Servings: 4

About this Recipe

A rich and creamy homemade Alfredo sauce that's perfect for pasta dishes. This classic Italian sauce is made with just a few simple ingredients but delivers an incredible depth of flavor. The combination of butter, heavy cream, and Parmesan cheese creates a luxurious sauce that coats pasta beautifully.

Ingredients

1/2 cup unsalted butter
1 cup heavy cream
1 cup freshly grated Parmesan cheese
2 cloves garlic, minced
1/4 tsp salt
1/4 tsp black pepper
1/4 tsp nutmeg (optional)
1 lb fettuccine pasta (for serving)

Instructions

1

Bring a large pot of salted water to boil for the pasta.

2

In a large skillet or saucepan, melt the butter over medium heat.

3

Add the minced garlic and cook for about 1 minute, until fragrant but not browned.

4

Pour in the heavy cream and bring to a gentle simmer. Cook for about 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.

5

Reduce the heat to low and gradually add the grated Parmesan cheese, stirring constantly until melted and smooth.

6

Season with salt, pepper, and nutmeg (if using). Taste and adjust seasoning as needed.

7

Meanwhile, cook the pasta according to package instructions until al dente.

8

Drain the pasta, reserving about 1/2 cup of the pasta water.

9

Add the cooked pasta to the sauce and toss to coat. If the sauce is too thick, add some of the reserved pasta water, a little at a time, until desired consistency is reached.

10

Serve immediately with extra Parmesan cheese and freshly ground black pepper.
