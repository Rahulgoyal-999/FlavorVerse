Sticky Gochujang Tofu with Herbs and Peanuts

Prep Time: 15 mins
Cook Time: 25 mins
Total Time: 40 mins
Servings: 4

A Korean-inspired dish featuring crispy tofu glazed with a sweet and spicy gochujang sauce, topped with fresh herbs and peanuts. This vegetarian dish is perfect for a quick weeknight dinner or as a side dish for a Korean feast.

Ingredients

  • 1 block firm tofu, pressed and cubed
  • 3 tablespoons gochujang paste
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons honey
  • 1 tablespoon rice vinegar
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon ginger, minced
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup roasted peanuts, chopped
  • Fresh herbs (cilantro, green onions)
  • Sesame seeds for garnish

Instructions

  1. Press the tofu for at least 30 minutes to remove excess water. Cut into 1-inch cubes.
  2. In a small bowl, whisk together gochujang, soy sauce, honey, rice vinegar, garlic, and ginger.
  3. Heat oil in a large non-stick skillet over medium-high heat.
  4. Add tofu cubes and cook until golden brown on all sides, about 10-12 minutes.
  5. Pour the sauce over the tofu and cook until the sauce thickens and coats the tofu, about 5 minutes.
  6. Remove from heat and garnish with chopped peanuts, fresh herbs, and sesame seeds.
  7. Serve hot with steamed rice or as a side dish.
